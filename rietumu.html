  • Year: 2020

About the Project

As the architect of this single-family residence, my goal was to design a home that blends with its natural surroundings and offers a peaceful atmosphere inside and out. The house is positioned to follow the sun, allowing the shadows of the trees to dance inside. By keeping the landscape natural and wild, I emphasized the forest atmosphere, making the home feel like a part of its environment. Inside, I used natural materials like wood and gray cement fiber boards to enhance the cozy, warm feeling. Large windows face southwest, filling the rooms with sunlight and providing beautiful views of the pine forest. Every design choice was made to create a harmonious living space that feels connected to nature, offering a serene retreat for its residents.

Concept

The single family residential house at 13 Rietumu Street, Carnikava, Latvia has acquired its unique form due to the shape of the land plot and the identity of the surrounding landscape.

The horizontal elements in the main facade contrast with the rhythm of the forest and at the same time create the impression of dynamic movement, whereas the facade on the courtyard side is dominated by vertical elements, which merges with the rhythm of the surrounding environment.

Site Plan

Atmosphere

The building's entrance faces north, while the terrace is bathed in the warm light of the southwest sun. From the terrace, one can enjoy a scenic view of the pine forest nestled deep within the plot. The living room windows also face southwest, ensuring ample sunlight and creating a pleasant atmosphere inside.

Sustainability is a key focus, reflected in the use of wooden structures and natural materials for the decor, such as stained wood finishing boards and gray cement fiber boards. The house is also equipped with an air recuperation system to enhance energy efficiency.
